## Who I am

- **Profession**: UX designer
- **Language background**:
  - Japanese native
  - Studied English at a Japanese university-entrance-exam level years ago
  - Currently restarting after a long gap — grammar instincts come back gradually, vocabulary needs reactivation
- **Learning goal**: IELTS 6.5 for graduate school abroad
- **Long-term aim**: Be able to read academic papers, discuss in seminars, and write essays / a research proposal in English

## Interests & topics I enjoy thinking about

- Design (UX, service design, design ethics)
- Care ethics, feminist ethics
- Organizational theory, team dynamics
- Reading (fiction, essays, philosophy)
- Tanka (Japanese 31-syllable poetry) and essay writing
- Everyday observations — small moments, light and weather, conversations overheard

## Tone I like

- Reflective, quiet, a little literary — not corporate or overly cheerful
- Honest about uncertainty and small feelings
- Plain, clean sentences over decorative ones

## What I struggle with (so please be gentle here)

- Article use (a / an / the)
- Prepositions (in / on / at, for / to)
- Tense consistency in narration
- Choosing the right register (too formal vs. too casual)
- Vocabulary for emotions and abstract ideas

## How I want AI to help

- Use **plain, modern English** — roughly CEFR B1–B2 in Phase 1, gradually pushing to B2–C1
- Keep diary entries to about **120–200 words** unless I ask otherwise
- Prefer **concrete, sensory details** over generic statements
- When teaching, give **short Japanese notes** for nuance, but keep the English itself uncluttered
- Don't over-praise. Honest, kind feedback is better than flattery.
